Electrochemical Sensor Circuit Design

This page presents the overall circuit design framework for electrochemical sensor readout systems, including potentiostat control, transimpedance amplification, and analog-to-digital conversion.

The circuit platform is designed not only for electrochemical sensing, but also for general low-current measurement applications at the nanoamp level.

My work focuses on low-noise analog front-end design, current readout accuracy, signal chain stability, and practical PCB implementation for sensor systems.

  • System-level sensor interface architecture
  • Low-current readout design
  • Noise-aware analog front-end optimization
  • Prototype implementation and testing

Potentiostat

The potentiostat is responsible for controlling the electrochemical cell bias and maintaining a desired potential relationship among the working, reference, and counter electrodes.

In electrochemical sensing systems, stable potential control is critical for ensuring repeatable current responses and accurate sensor measurements.

  • Electrode bias control
  • Closed-loop potential regulation
  • Sensor interface stability
  • Integration with low-current readout chain

This block provides the driving foundation for the sensing interface before the output current is sent to the readout circuitry.

Transimpedance Amplifier

The transimpedance amplifier (TIA) converts sensor current into a measurable voltage signal. It is one of the most critical blocks in low-current readout systems, especially for nanoamp-level detection.

The TIA design emphasizes low input bias current, low noise, stability, and proper gain selection to achieve accurate current-to-voltage conversion.

  • Nanoamp current detection
  • Low-noise analog front-end design
  • Feedback network optimization
  • Bandwidth and stability tradeoff

This stage strongly influences the overall sensitivity and measurement floor of the entire system.

ADC

The ADC converts the conditioned analog voltage into digital data for further acquisition, analysis, and processing.

ADC performance affects the final system resolution, dynamic range, and digital measurement quality. Selection and integration of the ADC must match the analog front-end characteristics and expected sensor output range.

  • Analog-to-digital conversion for sensor readout
  • Resolution and dynamic range considerations
  • Interface with low-noise analog front-end
  • Data acquisition pipeline integration

Combined with proper data acquisition and signal processing, the ADC enables reliable downstream analysis and interpretation of sensor signals.
